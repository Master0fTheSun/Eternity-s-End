The History of ZeilGalia

The Great War

Chronicle of the Burning Century

⌄

Tensions simmered between the East and West for hundreds of years before the Great War between them officially took hold. The conflict spanned a century of slaughter, devouring generations. It would force an industrial evolution fueled by blood and magic.

Here is the chronicle of the burning century.

I

The Spark and Blaze

4890 — 4900
4890

The Apex Incident

Catastrophe birthed itself in the breathless altitude of a Western high-tower vault. Vitaspark, unsheathed and ripe for the taking, flared with volatile power. The perfect prize for sixteen-year-old Lady Mynssa, who always had a hand for thieving. The niece of the King reached for the forbidden stone.

Fate would betray them all.

It was Magul Ragmal, an Eastern operative, who intercepted her grasp. Why he was there will forever remain a mystery—but the kinetic blast that ruptured the chamber was heard on the streets far below. Colored-glass rained over courtyard stones. Magul could only watch as Mynssa's plummeting body shattered against the masonry.

Within the day, Western magistrates branded him an assassin, and public outcry ensured the executioner's axe swiftly followed. The beheading of Magul Ragmal would spark a century-long conflict.

4891

The Eastern Retaliation

Vindication demanded salt and ash. A massive Eastern armada crested the horizon, bringing fire to the Western shores. Rapid, punitive blockades choked the ports, and raiding parties left coastal fortresses burning against the night sky.

4895

The First Convergence

The shadow war dragged into the light. Thousands clashed in the first large-scale military engagement of the era. Boots churned the soil into a thick, red mud. Heavy casualties on both sides proved that the skirmishes had mutated into a continental meat grinder.

4900

The Formal Declaration

The East officially declared total war. The realms locked their jaws around one another. Foundries roared to life, heavily militarizing the extraction and refinement of Ethergems to feed the insatiable engines of their expanding armies.

II

Demonic Escalation

4930 — 4950
4930

The Assault on Tombstone Crossing

Seeking to crack a decades-long stalemate, Eastern legions launched a brutal, grinding offensive against the Tulari. The strategic chokepoint of Tombstone Crossing ran red, dragging neutral factions into the widening gyre of slaughter.

4941

The Rise of Tur'Kang

Internal Eastern political strife culminated in a violent coup. The ruthless warlord Tur'Kang seized the Eastern throne. Scorning traditional military doctrine, he turned his gaze toward forbidden, apocalyptic arcana.

4945

The Unsealing of Killenden

Tur'Kang's vanguard smashed the ancient, glowing wards at Killenden. The earth shrieked as reality tore open, bridging the material plane with the Lower Realm. Dark, unpredictable energy flooded the surrounding valleys, wilting the flora and poisoning the aquifers.

4950

The Hell'Shuddin Pact

The Eastern warlord finalized a covenant with the abyss. Tur'Kang unleashed the Hell'Shuddin and swarms of Imps—relentless, demonic foot soldiers boasting unnatural resilience. His nightmare legions spilled onto the battlefields, dramatically breaking the Western lines.

III

The Cannibal Crown

4971 — 4985
4971

The Ascension of King Rufus Stalius

Rufus Stalius claimed the throne of Aleslydon. Pragmatic and utterly merciless, he immediately centralized all Western military authority under his direct command, forging a rigid iron shield against the demonic tide.

4972

The Naval Shift

Aleslydon's superior galleons crushed the Eastern fleet, sending burning timber to the ocean floor. In response, Eastern artificers deployed copper-plated submersibles and genetically warped aquatic beasts, dragging Western ships into the crushing depths.

4975

The Umbralith Siphon

Rufus required massive magical reserves to repel Tur'Kang's demons. He tightened a parasitic grip on Grey-Haven, manipulating trade routes to funnel their precious Umbralith directly into his own war machine. Varrhelm's high sages tracked the drain but swallowed their protests to preserve the fragile Western alliance.

4980

The Vanguard's Appointment

Captain Arthel Ziav, a brilliant and scarred tactician, assumed supreme command of the Aleslydon front. His mandate required holding the bleeding edge against the Hell'Shuddin.

4984

Arrival of The Seer

The Lower Realm birthed another nightmare. A shadowy entity known only as The Seer bypassed Tur'Kang, slipping into the Western courts to offer King Rufus dark counsel and the intoxicating poison of forbidden foresight.

4985

The Urion Rebellion

Drunk on the Seer's whispers, Rufus orchestrated the theft of the Blood-Weaver's Anchor Key. This catastrophic overreach triggered a violent uprising in Urion. The West began cannibalizing itself, bleeding out in civil war while simultaneously holding the Eastern front.

IV

Climax and Ash

4988 — 4995
4988

The March to Tombstone

Having crushed the Blood-Weaver rebellion under iron boot, the West unleashed its full might. Captain Ziav marched on Tur'Kang. The legendary Archknights of Kreelavee rode alongside them, joined by the resilient Tulari and the massive, scale-plated bulk of their giant desert serpent.

4989

The Battle of Tombstone Crossing

Magic and steel collided with demonic flesh in a world-shaking clash. Captain Ziav broke Tur'Kang's vanguard, sending the Eastern remnants crawling back toward the horizon. Amidst the carnage, a massive shadow eclipsed the sun. A dragon—the first seen in over three centuries—tore through the clouds, searing its legend into the surviving minds.

4990

The Chokehold on Grey-Haven

With the East broken, Rufus turned his hungry gaze inward. He deployed heavily armed battalions to encircle the mountains of Varrhelm, a silent threat suppressing any thought of post-war resistance.

4991

Ash Winter

Aleslydon's aggressive blockades severed vital mountain supply lines. A devastating, year-long famine gripped the high altitudes. Frost and starvation hollowed out the region, a tragedy etched into history as the Ash Winter.

4994

The Subjugation

Guided by The Seer, King Stalius completed his total dominance. Grey-Haven's Umbralith was strip-mined completely. The region withered into a husk, surviving solely on the charity of Varrhelm. Aleslydon rose from the ashes of its allies, glittering with unchallenged supremacy.

4995

The New Era

A full year bled away without a single Eastern soldier crossing the Tombstone borders. The Great War concluded. There was no grand celebration—only the heavy, mechanical grind of Aleslydon cementing its empire.

One hundred and five years. Four eras of blood. One empire standing on the bones of its allies.
